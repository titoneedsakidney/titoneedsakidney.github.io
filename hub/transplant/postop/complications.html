Post-Op: Complications

Most transplants do well. Problems are more manageable when caught early. Call if something feels wrong—trust that spidey sense.

Rejection (the immune response)

  • What it is: the immune system attacks the kidney. It can happen early or later, even years out.
  • How it’s found: rising creatinine, urine changes, symptoms, and sometimes a biopsy.
  • Treatment: medicines are adjusted; some cases need short infusions or a hospital stay.
  • Symptoms to report: fever, new pain over the kidney area, swelling, decreased urine, sudden weight gain.

Infections

  • Early (first month): surgical/line infections and typical hospital germs.
  • 1–6 months: you’re more vulnerable to certain viruses and opportunistic infections—report fevers and persistent cough/diarrhea.
  • After 6 months: risk shifts toward community infections (flu, COVID, stomach bugs). Keep vaccines updated and call early for antivirals if exposed.

Medication-related issues

  • High blood pressure, higher blood sugar or new diabetes, high cholesterol.
  • Tremor, headache, stomach upset/diarrhea.
  • Thinner bones over time; ask about vitamin D/calcium and screening.

Other potential problems

  • Urine/ureter issues: leaks or narrowings can cause pain, swelling, or low urine—these are fixable but need prompt care.
  • Blood clots: calf pain/swelling or shortness of breath needs urgent evaluation.
  • Skin cancers: more common on sun-exposed areas—do regular checks and use sunscreen.

When to call

  • Call your clinic promptly: fever > 100.4–101°F, chills, worsening cough, vomiting that prevents meds, new/worsening pain over the kidney, less urine, sudden weight gain.
  • Call 911 / go to ER: chest pain, trouble breathing, one-sided leg swelling/pain, severe dehydration, confusion, stroke-like symptoms.

Questions to ask: What are my usual “warning” patterns? If my labs rise, what tests come next? When should I go straight to the ER vs. call the clinic?

This page is educational and can’t cover every case. Your care team will personalize prevention and treatment.
