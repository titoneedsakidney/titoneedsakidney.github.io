Post-Op Staff: Social Worker

Role at a glance

  • Supports mental health, family/caregiver needs, work/benefits, and practical hurdles.
  • A safe place to talk through stress and big decisions.

They handle

  • Return-to-work planning, FMLA/disability paperwork, and employer notes.
  • Transportation, lodging, and financial-assistance resources.
  • Stress, anxiety, mood changes; referrals for counseling if needed.
  • Caregiver burnout support and community resources.

Not their job

  • They don’t give medical advice; they coordinate support and resources.

When to see them / what to bring

  • Anytime stress, finances, or logistics are getting in the way of recovery.
  • Bring forms, deadlines, and contact info for employers/insurers.

Questions to ask: What programs can help with travel, time off, or meds? How can my caregiver get support? Who do I call in a crisis?

Educational info; follow your team’s plan.
