Post-Op Staff: Transplant Pharmacist

Role at a glance

  • Makes your meds safe, effective, and doable in real life.
  • Teaches timing (e.g., “trough” levels before the morning dose) and interactions.

They handle

  • Checking drug levels; fine-tuning doses with your nephrologist.
  • Screening interactions (prescriptions, OTCs, supplements, certain citrus) and side-effect plans.
  • Refill coordination, prior auth support, and cost-saving alternatives when appropriate.

Not their job

  • They don’t change your treatment plan alone; adjustments are made with your clinic provider.

When to see them / what to bring

  • At discharge and early clinic visits; anytime you add/stop a med or supplement.
  • Bring every bottle (or clear list), dose times, and recent side effects.

Questions to ask: Which meds must stay on schedule to the minute? What should I avoid (foods/OTCs)? What do I do if a dose is late?

Educational info; follow your team’s plan.
