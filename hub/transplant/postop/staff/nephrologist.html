Post-Op Staff: Transplant Nephrologist

Role at a glance

  • Leads your medical care after transplant.
  • Tunes anti-rejection meds based on labs and symptoms.

They handle

  • Reviewing creatinine, drug levels, electrolytes, and blood pressure.
  • Diagnosing problems (dehydration, rejection, infection risks) and ordering tests/biopsy if needed.
  • Adjusting meds (anti-rejection, BP, diabetes, lipids) and follow-up plans.

Not their job (who does it instead)

  • Insurance logistics and forms → coordinator/social worker.
  • Day-to-day refills/PA chases → coordinator/pharmacy team.

When to see them / what to bring

  • Very frequent at first, then spaced out as things stabilize.
  • Bring a med list with exact times, home BP log, weights, and any symptoms.

Questions to ask: What are my lab targets right now? When should I call urgently vs. message? Any changes to my med times or doses?

Educational info; follow your team’s plan.
