Social Worker

Short Description

The dialysis social worker supports your emotional well-being and helps you navigate the practical challenges of living with kidney disease. They connect you to resources, counseling, and assistance programs.

Main Priorities

  • Provide emotional support and coping strategies for living with dialysis.
  • Help with applications for disability benefits, Medicare/Medicaid, and other assistance programs.
  • Connect patients with transportation services, support groups, and community resources.
  • Assist with advance care planning and legal paperwork if needed.

Issues They Work With

  • Emotional distress, anxiety, depression, or burnout related to dialysis.
  • Financial hardship, housing issues, or job loss connected to illness.
  • Arranging special accommodations for work or school while on dialysis.
  • Coordinating non-medical support services for patients and families.

When to Go to Them

  • If you feel overwhelmed, anxious, or depressed about your health situation.
  • When you need help finding transportation or financial assistance programs.
  • For guidance on disability applications or insurance paperwork.
  • If your living situation affects your ability to attend dialysis consistently.

What They Don’t Handle (Boundaries)

  • Making changes to your dialysis treatment or prescription.
  • Providing direct medical care or medication advice.
  • Scheduling your dialysis appointments or arranging equipment repairs.
