Dialysis Access Options

There are two main types of vascular access used for hemodialysis: catheters and fistulas. Some people may also have a graft, which is less common.

Catheters

Catheters are flexible tubes inserted into a large vein, usually in the chest. They’re often used when dialysis needs to begin quickly.

  • Quick to place, used for short-term access
  • Higher risk of infection and clotting
  • Requires special care and cleaning

Fistulas

A fistula is a connection made between an artery and a vein, usually in the arm. It takes a few months to heal but is the preferred access for long-term dialysis.

  • Lower infection risk
  • Lasts longer than a catheter
  • Needs time to "mature" before use

Grafts

A graft uses a soft tube to connect an artery to a vein. It’s used when veins aren’t suitable for a fistula.

  • Used when veins are too small or damaged
  • Heals faster than a fistula
  • Higher complication rate than a fistula

The best access depends on your health, vein quality, and how urgently dialysis is needed.
