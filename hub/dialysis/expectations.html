What to Expect During Dialysis

Starting dialysis can be overwhelming. Here’s what a typical in-center hemodialysis day looks like, so you know what to expect.

Arrival & Check-In

You’ll arrive at your scheduled time and check in at the front desk. A tech will weigh you and take your temperature, blood pressure, and other vitals.

Setup & Needle Insertion

A dialysis nurse or technician will prep your access site — cleaning it and inserting the needles (or connecting the catheter). This part can be uncomfortable, especially early on.

Treatment Time

Hemodialysis usually takes about 3.5 to 4 hours. You’ll sit in a recliner with your blood cycling through the machine. Most centers provide:

  • TVs or tablets for entertainment
  • Blankets (but you can bring your own)
  • Limited snacks or drinks, depending on policy

After Dialysis

Once the session ends, the nurse or tech will remove the needles and stop any bleeding. You’ll be weighed again, and your post-dialysis vitals will be recorded.

How You Might Feel

It’s common to feel tired, cold, or lightheaded after treatment. Some people need a nap, others bounce back quickly. Stay hydrated and watch for cramps or headaches — those may signal too much fluid was removed.

Over time, you’ll get used to the process. Every center runs a little differently, but most follow the same structure. Ask questions. Speak up if something doesn’t feel right.
